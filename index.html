About

Scope

We welcome work that treats theory as a guide to practice and practice as the test of theory: dialectics in biology and ecology, physics and chemistry, computation, the history of science, political economy, revolutionary strategy, anti-imperialist struggle, and the concrete analysis of concrete conditions.

Editorial principles

  • Clarity over jargon. Write for serious readers outside your subfield.
  • Method over mystique. Explain how claims were produced and what they predict.
  • Materialism. Locate ideas in history, production, and class relations.
  • Internationalism. Center the oppressed and colonized; oppose chauvinism.

This site is intentionally simple. All content lives in a small JavaScript file. To publish new work, you edit one list and commit.

Submit

How to submit

  1. Write your piece as plain text or Markdown.
  2. Include: title, author name(s), date, and 3–6 tags.
  3. Send it by email with “Synthesis submission” in the subject.

Email the editors

Tip: if you don’t want to use email, you can also publish by opening a “pull request” on GitHub.

Format & review

  • We publish: articles, notes, reviews, translations, and rebuttals.
  • Prefer citations and reproducible methods for empirical claims.
  • Clearly separate evidence, inference, and political conclusions.
  • Peer review can be open (signed) or anonymous—author’s choice.

This website does not collect personal data. If you link to external tools (forms, analytics), you’re choosing to add that infrastructure.
